## Design Context

### Users
ClueXP serves customers requesting urgent access services, field technicians responding
under time pressure, provider organizations coordinating teams, and internal dispatch
operators. Interfaces must support fast decisions, one-handed mobile use, clear operational
status, and trustworthy handling of identity, location, payment, and assignment details.

### Brand Personality
Professional, urgent, and trustworthy. ClueXP should feel like a mature dispatch network:
direct and calm under pressure, never playful, decorative, or like a generic SaaS template.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use ClueXP's black, white, neutral, and amber visual identity with strong typography, crisp
borders, restrained shadows, compact radii, and high contrast. Mobile field experiences are
map-first and status-driven; intake is calm and guided; operations consoles are dense and
scannable. Preserve the established design system and avoid copying third-party branding,
oversized marketing composition, decorative gradients, glass effects, and nested cards.

### Design Principles
1. Make the next safe action obvious and keep operational status visible.
2. Reveal sensitive customer or technician information only when the trust state permits it.
3. Design for touch, one-handed use, safe areas, text expansion, and unreliable networks.
4. Use amber selectively for urgency and primary action; keep the neutral foundation disciplined.
5. Prefer concise, factual copy and resilient loading, empty, retry, and terminal states.
